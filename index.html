Zimbabwe, recently featured on Forbes' "Best Places to Visit in 2025" list, is a country rich with cultural heritage and natural wonders. The nation preserves an impressive collection of 137 national monuments that showcase its diverse history, from ancient civilizations to colonial architecture and sacred natural sites.

These protected monuments represent Zimbabwe's commitment to conserving its unique cultural identity and breathtaking landscapes for future generations.

Zimbabwe's Climate Seasons

Plan your trip according to these seasons:

Season Months Characteristics
Wet Season November to March Hot and humid, with frequent rainfall, often in the form of afternoon thunderstorms.
Dry Season April to October Generally cooler and drier, with sunny but cold evenings and early mornings, especially from June to August.
Hot Dry Season August to October A period of progressively hotter and drier weather leading up to the return of the rains in November.

Top 5 Tourist Attractions

1. Victoria Falls

Known locally as "Mosi-oa-Tunya" (The Smoke That Thunders), this is one of the world's largest and most spectacular waterfalls, forming the border between Zimbabwe and Zambia.

2. Great Zimbabwe Ruins

The ancient stone city that gave the country its name, featuring impressive medieval architecture and serving as a testament to an advanced African civilization.

3. Hwange National Park

Zimbabwe's largest game reserve, spanning over 14,600 square kilometers and home to one of Africa's largest elephant populations along with diverse wildlife.

4. Mana Pools National Park

A UNESCO World Heritage site known for its wildlife concentrations and beautiful landscape along the Zambezi River, offering exceptional walking safaris.

5. Eastern Highlands

A mountainous region along the Mozambique border featuring stunning scenery, tea plantations, and some of Zimbabwe's best hiking trails through misty forests.
